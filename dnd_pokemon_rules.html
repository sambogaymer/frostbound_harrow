Trainer's Rulebook

Core D&D 5e rules — reskinned for the Pokémon world

Ability Scores

The Six Trainer Stats

Every trainer is defined by six core ability scores. These describe their natural physical and mental gifts:

Strength (STR) — Raw physical power. Used for melee attacks, grappling, and breaking things.
Dexterity (DEX) — Agility and reflexes. Used for ranged attacks, stealth, and light armor defense.
Constitution (CON) — Endurance and vitality. Affects your HP and ability to resist physical hardship.
Intelligence (INT) — Memory, reasoning, and knowledge. Used for Pokédex recall and tactical thinking.
Wisdom (WIS) — Perception and instinct. Used for reading Pokémon, sensing danger, and healing.
Charisma (CHA) — Force of personality. Used for leadership, performance, intimidation, and Pokémon bonding.

Ability Modifiers

Each score generates a modifier — the number you actually add to dice rolls. Scores below 10 give negative modifiers.

Proficiency Bonus

Trainer Experience Bonus

Your Proficiency Bonus represents your trainer experience — the edge you have with skills, tools, and attacks you've actually trained in. All trainers start at +2 at Level 1.

LEVEL 1–4 → +2  |  LEVEL 5–8 → +3  |  LEVEL 9–12 → +4

You add your proficiency bonus to:

— Attack rolls with weapons you're proficient with
— Ability checks using a skill you're proficient in
— Saving throws you're proficient in
— Spell attack rolls (if applicable)

You never add your proficiency bonus twice to the same roll, even if two rules say you should.

Advantage & Disadvantage

Double Roll Rule

Some situations give you Advantage or Disadvantage on a roll. These represent unusually favorable or unfavorable circumstances — like a Pokémon using Double Team, or fighting in thick snowfall.

Advantage: Roll two d20s and take the higher result.

Disadvantage: Roll two d20s and take the lower result.

Cancellation: If you have both Advantage and Disadvantage on the same roll (from any source), they cancel out exactly — you roll just one d20 regardless of how many sources you have of each.

Ability Checks

Making a Check

When the outcome of an action is uncertain, the Game Master calls for an ability check. Roll a d20, add the relevant ability modifier (and proficiency bonus if applicable), and compare to the Difficulty Class (DC).

d20 + MODIFIER (+ PROFICIENCY if applicable) vs. DC

Meet or beat the DC = success. Below the DC = failure.

Difficulty Classes

The GM sets the DC based on how hard the task is:

Contests

When two creatures oppose each other directly — a trainer trying to grab a Pokémon while another trainer tries to stop them — both make ability checks. The higher result wins. Ties go to the creature that initiated the contest.

Saving Throws

Resisting Effects

A saving throw is a reflexive check to resist or avoid a harmful effect — a Poison Powder, a collapsing ice shelf, a psychic attack. You don't choose to make one; effects force them.

d20 + ABILITY MODIFIER (+ PROFICIENCY if proficient) vs. effect DC

Each class grants proficiency in two saving throws. Your trainer's instincts, discipline, or sheer stubbornness gives them an edge against certain effects.

In the Frostbound Harrow: CON saves are common against the biting cold. WIS saves appear when the corruption spreads. Be ready for both.

Initiative

Battle Order

When combat begins, everyone rolls Initiative to determine the order of turns. Roll a d20 and add your DEX modifier. Higher results act first. Ties are broken by higher DEX score, then by coin flip if still tied.

INITIATIVE = d20 + DEX MODIFIER

Initiative lasts for the entire encounter. The GM tracks the order and calls each combatant's turn when it comes around.

Movement & Speed

Moving on Your Turn

On each turn, you can move up to your Speed — for most trainers, that's 30 feet. You can split your movement before and after your action.

Difficult terrain (deep snow, ice patches, tangled roots) costs 2 feet of movement per 1 foot traveled. Climbing and swimming also typically cost extra movement unless you have a relevant ability.

Special Movement

Jumping: Long jump = STR score in feet (running) or half that (standing). High jump = 3 + STR modifier in feet (running).
Crawling: Costs 1 extra foot per foot moved.
Falling: Take 1d6 bludgeoning damage per 10 feet fallen (max 20d6). You land Prone.

Actions in Battle

Attack

Make one melee or ranged weapon attack (or more if your class allows multiple). Roll to hit, then roll damage on a success.

Dash

Gain extra movement equal to your Speed for this turn. Useful for closing distance or fleeing across open terrain.

Disengage

Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attacks for the rest of this turn. Use this to safely retreat from a melee Pokémon.

Dodge

Until your next turn, attacks against you are made with Disadvantage if you can see the attacker. You also have Advantage on DEX saving throws. Requires you aren't Incapacitated and still have movement speed.

Help

Aid another creature with a task. If helping with an attack, your ally has Advantage on their next attack against a creature within 5 ft of you. If helping with a check, your ally has Advantage on that check.

Hide

Make a Stealth check to conceal yourself. You must be out of clear sight. If you succeed, you are Hidden — attackers have Disadvantage to hit you, and you have Advantage on your first attack from hiding.

Ready

Prepare an action to trigger on a specific condition: "When the Snover charges, I fire my bow." Specify both the trigger and the action. If the trigger occurs before your next turn, you use your Reaction to perform the action.

Search

Devote your attention to finding something — a hidden Pokémon, a concealed trap, or a clue. Make a Perception or Investigation check (GM decides which).

Use an Object

Use a Potion, throw a Poké Ball, activate a special item. Normally interacting with one object is free during movement; a second requires this action.

You also have a Bonus Action and a Reaction each round, granted by specific class features, spells, or situations. Your Reaction recharges at the start of each of your turns.

Attack Rolls

Hitting Your Target

Roll a d20 and add your attack bonus. If the total meets or beats the target's Armor Class (AC), the attack hits.

d20 + ABILITY MOD + PROFICIENCY BONUS vs. TARGET AC

Use STR for melee weapons, DEX for ranged weapons. Finesse weapons (like rapiers) let you choose either.

Critical Hits & Misses

Natural 20 (Critical Hit): Automatically hits, regardless of AC. Roll all damage dice twice and add modifiers once.

Natural 1 (Critical Miss): Automatically misses, regardless of bonuses. No damage is dealt.

Ranged Attacks

Ranged weapons have two range values (e.g., 80/320 ft). Within normal range: no penalty. Beyond normal range but within long range: Disadvantage. Beyond long range: impossible.

Firing while a hostile creature is within 5 ft of you also causes Disadvantage — it's hard to aim when something's biting your arm.

Damage & Types

Dealing Damage

On a hit, roll the weapon's damage dice and add your ability modifier (STR for melee, DEX for ranged/finesse). On a Critical Hit, roll the damage dice twice.

DAMAGE = DICE ROLL + ABILITY MODIFIER

Healing

Healing restores HP up to your maximum. You can't be healed above your max HP by normal means. A creature at 0 HP is Unconscious (fainted).

Conditions

Status effects that alter what a creature can do. Most end on their own or with specific treatment. Pokémon-world flavor shown in italics.

Blinded — "Flash'd"

  • Can't see; automatically fails checks requiring sight
  • Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage
  • The creature's attack rolls have Disadvantage

Charmed — "Attracted"

  • Can't attack the charmer or target them with harmful effects
  • The charmer has Advantage on CHA checks against the creature

Frightened — "Spooked"

  • Disadvantage on checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is in sight
  • Can't willingly move closer to the source of fear

Grappled — "Trapped"

  • Speed becomes 0; can't benefit from speed bonuses
  • Ends if the grappler is Incapacitated, or if the creature escapes (Athletics/Acrobatics vs. grappler's Athletics)

Incapacitated — "Stunned"

  • Can't take actions or reactions

Invisible — "Vanished"

  • Impossible to see without special senses
  • Attacks against the creature have Disadvantage
  • The creature's attacks have Advantage

Paralyzed — "Badly Paralyzed"

  • Incapacitated; can't move or speak
  • Automatically fails STR and DEX saves
  • Attacks against the creature have Advantage
  • Any hit within 5 ft is a Critical Hit

Petrified — "Stoned"

  • Transformed into solid matter; incapacitated and can't move or speak
  • Weight increases tenfold; immune to poison and disease (but existing ones are suspended)
  • Advantage on attack rolls against the creature; auto-fails STR and DEX saves

Poisoned — "Badly Poisoned"

  • Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks

Prone — "Knocked Down"

  • Only movement option is to crawl (costs 1 extra foot per foot) or stand up (costs half Speed)
  • Disadvantage on attack rolls
  • Melee attacks against the creature have Advantage; ranged attacks have Disadvantage

Restrained — "Bound"

  • Speed becomes 0
  • Disadvantage on attack rolls and DEX saves
  • Attacks against the creature have Advantage

Stunned — "Dazed"

  • Incapacitated; can't move; can speak only falteringly
  • Automatically fails STR and DEX saves
  • Attacks against the creature have Advantage

Unconscious — "Fainted"

  • Incapacitated; can't move or speak; unaware of surroundings
  • Drops held items; falls Prone
  • Automatically fails STR and DEX saves
  • Attacks against the creature have Advantage
  • Any hit within 5 ft is a Critical Hit

Exhaustion

Trainer Fatigue

Exhaustion accumulates from forced marching, starvation, extreme cold, and certain effects. It has 6 levels — level 6 is death. Each Long Rest removes one level of exhaustion.

LevelEffect
1Disadvantage on all ability checks
2Speed halved
3Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws
4HP maximum halved
5Speed reduced to 0
6Death

Cold weather: Without proper gear, a trainer may need to make a DC 10 CON save each hour in extreme cold or gain a level of Exhaustion. The Harrow is not forgiving.

Resting

Short Rest — "Catching Your Breath"

A period of at least 1 hour of downtime. You can eat, tend wounds, and let your Pokémon recover slightly. During a Short Rest you may spend Hit Dice to recover HP:

SPEND 1 HIT DIE → roll it + CON MOD → regain that many HP

You have a number of Hit Dice equal to your level. Spent Hit Dice are recovered on a Long Rest (regain half your total, rounded up).

Long Rest — "Setting Up Camp"

At least 8 hours of rest, including at least 6 hours of sleep. You must have at least 1 HP to benefit. After a Long Rest:

— Regain all lost HP
— Regain spent Hit Dice (up to half your total, minimum 1)
— Remove 1 level of Exhaustion
— Restore class resources (spell slots, etc.)

You can only benefit from one Long Rest per 24 hours. Interruptions of 1+ hour of strenuous activity reset the rest.

Fainting & Death

Dropping to 0 HP — "Fainted"

When your HP reaches 0, you fall Unconscious. You are now dying. Each turn you are dying, you make a Death Saving Throw:

Roll d20 — 10 or higher = Success  |  9 or lower = Failure

— 3 successes: You become Stable (unconscious but no longer dying)
— 3 failures: You die
— Natural 20: You regain 1 HP and regain consciousness
— Natural 1: Counts as two failures
— Taking damage at 0 HP: Counts as one failure. A Critical Hit counts as two.

Stabilizing a Downed Trainer

A creature that reaches you can use their Action to attempt a DC 10 Medicine check to stabilize you. Success means you stop making Death Saves and remain unconscious.

A Healer's Pouch, Hyper Potion, or magical healing also stabilizes and may restore HP immediately.

Instant Death

If damage reduces you to 0 HP and there is remaining damage equal to or greater than your HP maximum, you die instantly — no Death Saving Throws.

In the Frostbound Harrow: The cold and the corruption are real threats. Keep your HP up and your Healer's Pouch close.
